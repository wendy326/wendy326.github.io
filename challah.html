WENDY SUN

Challah

Ingredients

    Tangzhong
    • 1 1/4 cup warm water (110 degrees F)
    • 2 1/4 tbsp yeast
    • 6 tbsp or 1/4 + 1/8 cup honey
    • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
    • 3 eggs
    • 3/4 tsp salt
    • 4 cups bread flour
    • (optional) sesame seeds for topping

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, proof the yeast for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Beat in the honey, oil, 2 eggs and one yolk, and salt.
  3. Add flour one cup at a time, beating then kneading after each addition.
  4. Knead until smooth and elastic and no longer sticky, adding flour as needed.
  5. Cover with a damp clean cloth and let rise for 1 1/2 hours or until dough has doubled in bulk.
  6. Gently punch dough down and knead a bit, then separate it into 6 equal pieces.
  7. Shape each piece into a long rope, then pinch the ends of the 6 pieces firmly together.
  8. Braid the loaf.
  9. Place loaf on a greased baking tray, cover with towel(s), then let rise for an hour.
  10. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F.
  11. Beat the remaining egg white and brush over the braid, sprinkling sesame seeds over top.
  12. Bake for 20 minutes, rotating it in the middle if needed.
  13. Cover loaf with aluminum foil to prevent burning and bake for another 10 minutes
  14. Take loaf out of the oven and immediately brush tops with melted butter.
  15. Let rest for an hour and enjoy!

This is a big loaf of bread (16'' x 5.5'' x 2.5''), so plan equipment and storage space accordingly. When measuring the flour, make sure that you sift it before measuring out the 4 cups, since sifting will change the density. If the dough looks dry, let rise/rest double the time. You can add another egg to the dough for eggier flavor. My bread was not very sweet, so you could add 1/2 cup honey instead of .375 cup like I did. There are multiple ways to braid the bread, I liked using six strands, but you can use anywhere from 3 to 9, as demonstrated in the video linked below the recipe.
