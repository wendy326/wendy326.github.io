WENDY SUN

Dan Dan Noodles

Ingredients

    Meat and Zha Cai
    • 3 tsp vegetable oil
    • 8 oz ground pork
    • 2 tsp hoisin or sweet bean sauce
    • 2 tsp cooking wine
    • 1 tsp dark soy sauce
    • 1/2 tsp chinese five spice powder
    • 1/3 cup finely chopped preserve Sichuan Vegetable (zha cai)
    Sauce
    • 2 tbsp sesame paste
    • 3 tbsp soy sauce
    • 2 tsp sugar
    • 1/4 tsp chinese five spice powder
    • 1/2 tsp chili flakes
    • 1/2 cup chili oil
    • 2 cloves garlic, finely minced
    • 1/4 cup hot cooking water from noodles
    Noodles and Greens
    • 1 lb fresh or dried white noodles
    • 1 small bunch leafy greens
    • chopped peanuts
    • chopped scallion

Directions

  1. In a wok, heat a tsp of oil over medium heat and brown the pork.
  2. Add sweet bean/hoisin sauce, cooking wine, dark soy sauce, and five spice powder.
  3. Cook until all the liquid has evaporated, then set aside.
  4. Heat other 2 tsp of oil in wok over medium heat and sautee the zha cai for a few minutes, then set aside.
  5. Cook noodles according to package directions and set aside, saving 1/4 cup water for sauce.
  6. For the sauce, combine all the sauce ingredients and adjust as needed.
  7. Blanch the greens in the noodle water and drain.
  8. Transfer noodles to serving bowl and top with pork, zha cai, and greens. Spoon sauce onto the noodles.
  9. Sprinkle with peanuts and scallions.

Chili oil: I just used lao gan ma + vegetable oil + some chinese five spice powder. The original recipe has a recipe for a spicy version. A lot of these measurements are highly adjustable. In the image, I used chicken breast instead of ground pork, which worked fine.
