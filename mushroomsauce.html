Creamy Mushroom Sauce

Ingredients

  • salt and pepper
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, quartered and thinly sliced
  • 8 oz (1 pkg) sliced mushrooms
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup milk

Directions

  1. Put butter, oil, thyme, a pich of salt and pepper, onion, and mushrooms to a skillet and saute over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally, until mushrooms are browned (5-10 min)
  2. Add flour and stir to coat, then cook for another minute
  3. Gradually pour in the chicken broth
  4. Stir and continue to cook over medium heat, until sauce has thickened
  5. Stir in milk
  6. Remove from heat and season as needed
  7. Serve as a pasta sauce or with bread

The original recipe made this sauce with chicken, so meatballs or something would probably be a tasty addition. Also, the recipe said that you can use grated parmesan cheese to replace the flour as a thickening agent, but please remember that Walmart-brand grated parmesan cheese was not what they were talking about. It didn't help thicken up the sauce at all, and all the cheese kinda clumped together. Also it was too salty. I used portobello mushrooms, but I'm sure white mushrooms or whatever would work too. For the chicken broth, I made it using the concentrated powder, following the instructions on the box which worked fine. This recipe makes quite a bit, about 3.5 meals.
