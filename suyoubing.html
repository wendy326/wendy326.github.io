WENDY SUN

酥油饼

Ingredients

  • 1 bowl warm water
  • white sugar
  • baking soda
  • black sesame paste
  • oil
  • flour

Directions

  1. In a medium bowl, combine water, 1 tbsp oil, and baking soda
  2. Gradually add flour until the dough is not sticky, but still very soft
  3. Knead dough on a lightly floured surface and split into 2 inch diameter balls
  4. In a small bowl, make 油酥 using flour and oil
  5. Using a rolling pin, roll out the dough balls, then add a dollop of 油酥 to the center. Pinch the edges of the dough together like a baozi
  6. Flatten them with your palm and roll them out again with a rolling pin
  7. Roll each flattened pancake up into a log, like you would with a cinnamon roll
  8. Take the ends of each log and twist, like wringing out a towel, and [com]press it flat like a pop can
  9. Roll them out with the rolling pin again
  10. Place a dollop of your filling of choice (instructions below) in the center, and pinch the edges of the dough together again, just like before
  11. Flatten them with your palm and roll them out slightly, so they're about 1/2 an inch thick
  12. Brush the pancakes lightly with oil and place them on the griddle (~350 degrees F)
  13. Cook on the griddle until the outside looks slightly goden brown and the inside looks cooked

Fillings

There are a variety of options, here are some:

  1. Sugar
    • Mix together sugar, flour(half the amount of sugar), and oil
    • Note: 2/3 of the flour can be cooked in order to have more defined layers in the pancake
  2. Black Sesame
    • Mix together black sesame paste and sugar
  3. Savory Black Sesame
    • Mix together black sesame paste and salt... i think
  4. Peanut Butter
    • Mix together peanut butter and flour
    • Note: I haven't tried this myself, nor have I seen it tried before, so idk how much flour would be needed
  5. Nutella
    • Mix together nutella and flour
    • Note: I haven't tried this myself, nor have I seen it tried before, so idk how much flour would be needed
  6. Jam
    • Mix together jam and flour
    • Note: I haven't tried this myself, nor have I seen it tried before, so idk how much flour would be needed
    • Note pt 2: idk how well fruity flavors would work with this recipe. also, idk if the flour would work to thicken this up

I'm not sure what the exact quantities of each ingredient are, but I put the amount for the water and oil for the dough for a ratio. For the filling, remember that if it you are putting something very thin into the pancake or something that has a tendency to get thin/melt under heat, add some flour to thicken it up. Also, I personally prefer sweeter fillings, so I've inclulded more of those, but I'm sure that there's a lot more savory ones.
